Lamborghini

Lamborghini is an Italian manufacturer of super sports cars founded in 1963 by the tractor manufacturer Ferruccio Lamborghini, with headquarters and offices located in the city of Sant'Agata Bolognese, in the Bologna region and currently part of the Volkswagen Group (VAG Group). Some of the names of their cars refer to names of pardoned fighting bulls, such as Murciélago, famous or historical ones such as Diablo or Aventador, or simply words related to bullfighting such as Miura, Espada or Urraco.

Lamborghini Miura

Produced between 1966 and 1972, the Miura was a mid-engine two-seater that had three variants: P400, S and SV. In the P400, the 3.9 V12 engine produced 350 HP, while the S was a restyling that also delivered 20 HP more, and the SV reached a power of 385 HP.
